My Manifesto: From Designer to Product Forger

I am a designer. I have spent years crafting products that live on screens, but I reached a breaking point. I grew tired of the distance between the vision and reality, of the formulas that limit creativity, and of the rush that kills craftsmanship. My solution was not a new title, but a change in mindset. I decided that the only way to reclaim the craft in the digital world was to turn the vision into code myself.

Why This Isn't Just Another "Learning Journal"

This space is the record of my evolution, a live testimony of my transition to a Design Engineer. I am not here just to document what I learn, but to confront the industry's status quo. Every project and every article is part of my personal manifesto: a defense of intention, authenticity, and an obsession with detail. It is a challenge to the culture of the generic, and an invitation to build products that not only work, but also endure.
